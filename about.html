About Me

Hi, I’m Grace! My love for experience and product design began when I was accepted to the emerging technologies summer program at The School of The New York Times in high school. Working with my peers, I researched and designed artificial intelligence chatbots and self-driving cars, all while engaging with the innovative city of New York. I completely fell in love with human-centered design and the intersection of technology and people. The rest was history.


Now, I’m a second-year student at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor studying User Experience Design. My interests reside in inclusivity and ethical tech. My affinity for promoting diversity and inclusive design stems from my own identity and experiences in tech. As a second-generation Middle Eastern immigrant, I’ve found that my perspectives are sorely lacking in the realm of technology. True representation in technology will manifest much more efficiently if the people behind the scenes understand ethical concerns firsthand and can enable avenues that creatively come up with solutions for disadvantaged populations.
